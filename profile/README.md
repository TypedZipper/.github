TypedZipper
One developer. AI as co-performer. Three interconnected open source projects for verified, inclusive civic infrastructure.
TypedZipper is an open civic infrastructure ecosystem built around three distinct but connected projects:
StaTutny — open protocol for verified civic data flows
VUHO — open hardware edge device for verified human presence
FELIS — open methodology for AI-assisted formal system design
Together, they address a shared problem: civic systems break when signals, decisions, accountability, and human reality are disconnected.
---
The Problem
Modern civic life — housing governance, social care, community accountability — runs on broken infrastructure:
incident reports arrive via chat with no evidentiary weight
governance decisions happen separately from documented facts
elderly and vulnerable people have no dignified, privacy-respecting way to signal their status to caregivers
solo developers building serious open systems have no formal methodology for AI-assisted architecture
These are not UI problems. They are protocol problems.
---
The Ecosystem
StaTutny — Open Protocol for Verified Civic Data Flows
A formal, auditable chain between:
```text
signal → incident → decision → commitment → confirmation → legal act
```
Built on the Typed Seam Engine (TSE) — a domain-agnostic open source semantic core. Each seam between layers is a first-class, machine-verifiable contract.
Real pilots: Yaroslavska 32/33 (Kyiv) and Krystal-19 housing cooperative.  
Jurisdiction: Ukraine (JurisdictionPack.UA.Housing.v0.2 — active draft).  
Status: Full specification corpus complete. Implementation in progress.
---
VUHO — Open Hardware Edge Device for Verified Human Presence
An ESP32-P4 based open hardware terminal that generates verified, typed presence and activity signals.
In the StaTutny ecosystem, VUHO serves as the terminal for social care — elderly people living alone, people with disabilities, patients under home care. Not surveillance. A consent-based, privacy-respecting signal with formal provenance.
Status: Hardware profile, temporal execution model, and primitive matrix formally documented.
---
FELIS — Open Methodology for AI-Assisted Formal System Design
The methodology and design environment used to build StaTutny and VUHO. FELIS enables a single developer with AI as co-architect to deliver systems with the formal rigor of a large team — without losing architectural integrity or human control.
Status: Actively used. Documentation in progress.
---
Why This Matters
> 70,000+ housing cooperatives and HOAs in Ukraine have no formal accountability infrastructure.  
> Millions of elderly and vulnerable people have no dignified digital presence in civic systems.  
> Thousands of solo developers building serious open infrastructure have no formal AI-assisted methodology.
This ecosystem addresses all three gaps with open protocols, open hardware, and open methodology.
